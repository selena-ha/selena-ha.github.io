MedPak

Access medical documents digitally.

Role: UX Designer, UX Researcher

Duration: 7 months

Tools: Google Survey, Marvel, Zoom, Slack, Usability Hub, Figma, Optimal Workshop

Background

The increase of medical and healthcare apps and platforms, while offering diverse benefits, can create confusion and challenges for individuals trying to stay on top of their health demands. MedPak will provide users the confidence to stay on top of their health and wellness information. Users will have access to medical documents, manage appointments and learn more about health and wellness information.

Problem

Patient’s health data and documents are scattered across these platforms, making it difficult to access a comprehensive overview of one’s health status.

Objective

Simplifying access across these platforms could alleviate the confusion and difficulties associated with tracking medical documentation and information spread across multiple apps. The app aims to relieve stress when managing documents and appointments. Users will feel in charge of their health and enhance their wellbeing and lifestyle.

How I got there

RESEARCH

Competitive Analysis

In response to the recent pandemic, online medical services were accepted quickly as in-person visits were limited. It has reshaped the way healthcare is delivered and accessed by individuals. To take a look at how the online medical services are employed, I did a competitive analysis to identify the things that are going well and any pain points users are experiencing.

DESIGN

Usability Testing

With the mid-fidelity prototypes, I conducted a moderated remote and in-person usability test. Six test participants were recruited from my personal network to understand any difficulties the user may have when navigating to their goal. In each session, the participants are given a set of scenarios and asked to complete the desired task.

Objectives

  1. If participants are able to access the document and share it with a doctor.
  2. Measure how users would find a doctor.
  3. Can participants book an appointment with the chosen doctor?
  4. How will the participants access resources and articles?

Usability Testing Analysis

After organizing the data from the usability testing, I extracted and grouped information collaboratively using four groups: Observations, Positive Quotes, Negative Quotes and Errors. Then I used a Rainbow Spreadsheet to visualize unseen patterns and hierarchies that had emerge. I was able to pinpoint the key problems that users were having and looked into possible solutions.

Issues and Solutions

The following are the key problems that were identified during the analysis and their solutions to improve overall user experience.

Problems
  1. Users had a hard time “Adding a new doctor” to their profile.
  2. Users assumed the Account tab was for settings features and had hard time finding personal information.
  3. Users felt the bookmarked resources in the Account tab to be unnatural.
Solutions

Make the search bar functional and distinct. Ensure the language is clear by inputting titles and placeholders.

Change the location of bookmarked resources to the Resource tab for better grouping and navigation.

Change the titles within the Account tab for better understanding.
